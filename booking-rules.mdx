The same rules apply whether you book in the web app, the CLI, or through an agent. They are enforced on the server, so no client can work around them.

## The rules

| Rule | What it means |
|---|---|
| Time model | Book any free time range, in 15-minute steps. |
| Minimum duration | 15 minutes. |
| Daily budget | Up to **2 hours** of booking per person per Brussels calendar day. Admins are exempt. |
| Booking horizon | Up to **30 days** ahead, never in the past. |
| Opening hours | Between **06:00 and 22:00** Europe/Brussels, within a single day. Applies when you create or edit a booking; bookings made before this rule existed stay valid, and admin maintenance blocks are exempt. |
| Edit | Change your own booking until it starts. |
| Cancel | Cancel your own booking until it ends, so an in-progress booking you no longer need frees the room. |
| Privacy | Others see your **name** on a booking, never its **title**. Only you and admins see the title and the guest list. |
| Guests | Invite up to **10 people by email** when you book — any address works, guests don't need a WAT account. They get the invitation, the reminder, and any updates or cancellations. |

<Note>
The daily budget counts only your **active** bookings. Cancel one and that time frees up again for the day.
</Note>

## Inviting guests

Add guest email addresses when you create (or edit) a booking — in the web app's booking panel, with the CLI's `--guests` flag, or through an agent. Guests don't need a WAT account: any email address works.

- Each guest receives an **invitation email** with a calendar invite (`.ics`), worded for them ("<booker> invited you"), plus the **reminder** before the meeting and any **update or cancellation** emails.
- Editing the guest list later invites the newcomers and sends removed guests a cancellation of their invite (their calendar entry clears, nobody else's does).
- The guest list is visible only to you and admins, like the title.
- Limit: 10 guests per booking.

## Email notifications

| Email | Who | When | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Login code | You | Each time you log in | The 6-digit one-time code |
| Booking confirmation | You | When you create a booking | A calendar invite (`.ics`) so you can add it to your own calendar, plus the guest list |
| Guest invitation | Your guests | When you book (or add them later) | "<booker> invited you" with the same calendar invite |
| Cancellation notice | You + your guests | When a booking is cancelled, including by an admin | The cancelled booking's details + a calendar cancellation |
| Update notice | You + your guests | When the time or room changes | The new time |
| Reminder | You + your guests | Shortly before the booking starts | The upcoming booking's details |

You control **your own** confirmation and reminder emails (and the reminder lead time) on the **Notifications** tab of the **Account** page in the web app — guests always receive theirs. The **API keys** tab on the same page manages the keys your CLI and agents use.

## Admins

WAT admins manage everything in the web app: they create, edit, and archive rooms, block rooms (these show as "Unavailable" on the calendar), view or cancel any booking, and manage members. Admin blocks take priority: if a block covers a time you had booked, your booking is cancelled automatically — it moves to your cancelled list and you receive an email asking you to reschedule. Blocks are exempt from the daily budget, and admins are exempt from it on their own bookings too. Most members never need the admin area.

Admins can also set **recurring blocks**: a standing weekly unavailability on a room (for example, every Monday 12:00–20:00). These show as "Unavailable" on the calendar like any other block, week after week, until an admin removes the rule. The same priority applies: bookings inside an upcoming recurring window are cancelled automatically and their owners emailed.
